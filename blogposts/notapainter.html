The links are at the bottom, but I wrote some very brief thoughts on how to reconcile wanting to be a writer but hating to share the things I’ve written.

Feel free to skip to the bottom

I’ve been keeping a journal for over two years now. I don’t hold myself to any strict schedules on when I should write or what I should write about, so the notebook only has the thoughts I felt were too overwhelming to keep stored up in my head. I had finally read through some of the first entries a few months ago and was pretty disappointed. I couldn’t go more than a few pages without finding a new list of goals, actionable plans and timelines to achieve those goals. Inevitably there was an entry a few pages later that reads like a sober apology to my past self: I haven’t stuck to my timelines. I was too focused on the dumb unproductive things.

I’ve grown a lot since the days of “productivity or perish.” I don’t really view relaxing as a moral failure anymore[1], yet I find myself once again filling up journal entries with grandiose plans and timelines. I made a big list of literally everything I was interested in:

I want to write math explainers for magazines and get a job as a tutor and create a newsletter where I write about my objectively correct opinions on contemporary discourse in politics and culture and I want to get yolked and have a specific friend group just to make art with and I want to play the saxophone and…[2]

I cut back a bit on the scope and made step-by-step plans for writing specifically since I think I may want a career or something in it. That was a month ago (New Years, y’know). I haven’t really made good on those plans, like at all.

I have an odd mental block when it comes to sharing things I make. If I’m writing for myself and I know that all the words I mush together into sentences will remain in some folder on my computer, I have no problem putting thousands, creating a couple hundred kilobytes worth of words. Yet, every time I start to write something I plan on sharing — something that will escape the obscurity of my (C:) drive — I lose faith in my skills and let it waste away in a sub-folder for things I will share “one day.”

My painting is dead.
Defend it for me, Giovanni, protect my honor.
I am not in the right place—I am not a painter.
— Michelangelo (1509)

I heard about this quote while listening to a podcast in the shower and I read about it further over breakfast. This is how Michelangelo ended a letter he wrote to a friend while he was slaving away painting the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel — a rare work of art so technically impressive and visually stunning that it has been placed well within both the artistic cannon and the common knowledge of non-artists.

Now, I don’t want to draw any false equivalences here… but I should probably get over myself and just start publishing things. Even one of the greatest artists in history felt like an impostor while creating his magnum opus. I really can’t afford to be stressing over making something perfect. I’m writing about things I think are cool for an audience of no one, not getting commissioned by the Pope to paint the house of God.

I’m not sure why specifically this quote inspired me so much, but dissecting my inspiration is a surefire way to kill it. Instead, I’ll use it as an excuse to finally write something simple and low-stakes on the internet.

I can already feel myself trying to back out of writing this. A reminder for myself: the intended audience is not the paying patrons of some magazine or journal. This is for friends and family. This is not a lengthy opinion piece on what happened to Claudine Gay and the destruction of educational institutions nor a rambling, misguided review of Infinite Jest. Those pieces will remain locked up in the (C:) drive indefinitely.

This is a handful of links to things that I found interesting over the last week or so. Hopefully, I’ll find the courage to write more interesting things for those bigger audiences some day. For now, I’ll get over myself and finally start painting my incomparably shittier version of the Sistine Chapel.

Without further ado: here is my list of cool things I want to share.

Some things I found interesting:

  1. I really think the rest of the sonnet/letter that Michelangelo wrote is worth reading. Also, since it wasn’t written in English there are other translations from the original text.

  2. I first ran into Michelangelo’s letter when I was listening to the newest episode of what is quickly becoming my favorite podcast: Let’s Learn Everything! They have all the cute quirks of earnest people who are genuinely passionate about the world they found themselves in. I couldn’t recommend them more.

  3. The elevator in my building sucks. It sucks so bad that I had enough time to think about optimal elevator algorithms while I waited. Turns out, the optimal elevator is still being written about — this paper was published in June of last year. Also, the abstract version of the problem has a lot more application than elevators — its most obvious influence being the SCAN disk-scheduling algorithm. I plan on writing more about this once I get the chance to read it thoroughly.

  4. Your favorite mathematician’s[3] favorite digital audio workstation: Desmos! Not to be a GeoGebra hater, but Desmos hosts a yearly art competition (2023 winners here) and just over a year ago they added a tone function that essentially allows the calculator to function as a synthesizer. Here are some of my favorite compositions so far:[4]

    2. I think Desmos might be my new DAW...

    3. Desmos is my favorite Synthesizer...

    4. Desmos is the best DAW...

  5. I recently read Gabrielle Zevin’s novel Tomorrow and Tomorrow and Tomorrow and it was seriously really good. I wrote a more in-depth review — more than “it was really good” — but that is locked in deep under (C:). I’d say read the book, but a whole 400 page novel is a big ask for a list of links. The book is named after this soliloquy from an indie play you’ve probably never heard of, Macbeth. It is a much shorter read. I find it somehow both depressing and comforting. I, for one, am enjoying my time fretting upon the stage.

  6. Now, I said I wasn’t going to write about politics… BUT this is old enough to not have any direct relevance to modern-day politics. I don’t mean to out myself as a David Foster Wallace fan since it has a bit of a negative connotation in literary circles, but I found this piece on John McCain’s campaign to be something special. It is a glimpse into the grim reality of campaign trails and asks impossible questions about true character and proximity to power. It is a little long, but maybe toss it in the bookmarks for later.

  7. Not to sure why this is such a poetry-heavy list — I really didn’t think I was that into poetry — but my favorite rap collective Coast Contra is back in the studio and they uploaded what is best described as the only slam poetry I have ever enjoyed.

I think these are all the links I am going to include. I have some more stuff I think might be worth sharing but I’ll just toss that in a bookmarks folder and save it for if there is ever a “next time” I do this.

If anything, it was worthwhile to try and think about all the things I saw recently that I think would be fun to share. It’s kinda like an internet gratitude journal. More likely than not, I will make another list.

I hope you found at least one thing worth your time.

Thanks for reading.
